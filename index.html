FAQ

TRON AML Questions

Everything you need to know about TRON AML verification, TRX risk scoring, USDT TRC20 sanctions screening, and AMLCrystal Scanner's approach.

What is AMLCrystal Scanner?

AMLCrystal Scanner is a TRON blockchain AML (Anti-Money Laundering) compliance concept platform. It checks any TRX wallet address, USDT TRC20 address, or TRON transaction for AML risk, sanctions exposure, mixer usage, and financial crime indicators, presenting the results as a compliance-style report starting from $0.10 USDT per scan.

How does AMLCrystal Scanner work?

AMLCrystal Scanner is designed to analyze the on-chain TRON transaction history of a wallet or address, trace TRX fund flows up to 10 hops through the TRON blockchain graph, check against sanctions lists such as OFAC, EU and UN, detect mixer or obfuscation activity, and compute a composite AML risk score from 0 (clean) to 100 (critical high risk).

Which TRON tokens does AMLCrystal Scanner support?

AMLCrystal Scanner is built to support the TRON ecosystem: TRX (native TRON coin), USDT TRC20 (Tether on TRON), USDC TRC20, WTRX (Wrapped TRX), JST, SUN, BTT (BitTorrent), WIN, and other TRC20 standard tokens on the TRON Mainnet.

How much does a TRON AML scan cost?

Single TRON AML scans start at $0.10 USDT (pay-as-you-go). Bulk packs for 100+ TRON scans start at $0.05 per scan. TRON API plans for automated screening start at $0.02 per API call. Enterprise TRON plans with custom pricing are available for high-volume users.

What does the TRON AML risk score mean?

The TRON AML risk score is a composite number from 0 to 100. Scores of 0–25 indicate a clean, low-risk TRON address with no suspicious activity detected. Scores of 26–59 indicate medium risk requiring enhanced due diligence. Scores of 60–100 indicate high or critical risk requiring immediate compliance review.

Does AMLCrystal Scanner check USDT TRC20 addresses specifically?

Yes. USDT TRC20 is Tether's stablecoin on the TRON network and one of the most transacted crypto assets globally. AMLCrystal Scanner is designed to provide dedicated USDT TRC20 AML compliance checks that trace the TRON-layer transfer history of an address, including counterparty risk analysis and layering pattern detection.

What global sanctions lists does AMLCrystal Scanner screen against?

AMLCrystal Scanner's risk model is built around 15+ global sanctions and watchlists, including OFAC SDN List (USA), OFAC Consolidated Sanctions, EU Consolidated Sanctions, UN Security Council Sanctions, UK HM Treasury Sanctions, FATF High-Risk Jurisdictions, and additional TRON-specific crypto intelligence sources.

How accurate is AMLCrystal Scanner's TRON risk detection?

AMLCrystal Scanner targets 99.3% overall AML risk classification accuracy on TRON network addresses, validated against known TRON blockchain forensics case studies. Mixer and obfuscation detection targets 99.4% accuracy, with a goal of a 100% match rate for direct TRON address hits on OFAC and EU lists.

How long does a TRON AML scan take?

Standard TRON AML scans are designed to complete in under 2 seconds for most TRX wallets and USDT TRC20 addresses. TRON addresses with exceptionally large transaction histories may take longer for a full 10-hop deep-trace analysis. A report is generated as soon as the scan completes.

Does AMLCrystal Scanner offer a REST API for TRON?

AMLCrystal Scanner is designed around a REST API supporting single TRX and USDT TRC20 address scans, batch requests for large sets of TRON addresses, webhook alerts for monitored TRON wallets, transaction-level TRON AML checks, and bulk result export in JSON and CSV. The API would be available under the TRON API Plan and Enterprise tiers.

Who should use AMLCrystal Scanner?

Any business or individual sending or receiving TRX or USDT TRC20 on the TRON network can benefit from AML compliance checks. Key users include TRON-based crypto exchanges, USDT TRC20 payment processors, TRON DeFi platforms, TRON NFT marketplaces, OTC desks trading TRX, and compliance teams performing TRON blockchain due diligence.

Can AMLCrystal Scanner detect TRON mixer usage?

AMLCrystal Scanner's mixer detection concept covers known TRON mixing and obfuscation services, including TRON-native privacy tools and cross-chain bridge hops that obscure TRON fund origins. Mixer exposure is one of the highest-weighted risk factors in the TRON AML score, covering both direct mixer transactions and downstream exposure.

Is AMLCrystal Scanner aligned with FATF guidelines for TRON?

AMLCrystal Scanner's risk methodology is designed in line with FATF guidance on virtual asset risk assessment, including recommendations for virtual asset service providers (VASPs) operating on TRON. The concept aims to help TRON VASPs fulfill customer due diligence (CDD), enhanced due diligence (EDD), and FATF Travel Rule obligations.

What is direct vs indirect TRON sanctions exposure?

Direct TRON sanctions exposure means the checked TRX or USDT TRC20 address appears directly on a sanctions list such as OFAC SDN. Indirect TRON exposure means the wallet has transacted with a sanctioned TRON entity within the analyzed transaction graph. A full report would indicate hop distance, transaction amount, and fund flow direction for indirect exposures.

Can AMLCrystal Scanner analyze TRON DeFi wallets?

AMLCrystal Scanner is designed to support TRON DeFi wallet addresses, analyzing the TRON transaction history of wallets that interact with lending, swap, and yield protocols on TRON. For TRON smart contract addresses, the platform would provide counterparty risk analysis based on wallets that have funded or interacted with the contract.

How does real-time TRON monitoring work?

AMLCrystal Scanner's continuous monitoring concept subscribes a TRX or USDT TRC20 address for ongoing surveillance on the TRON Mainnet. The engine would monitor incoming and outgoing TRON transactions as they occur on-chain, triggering an alert via email or webhook when a monitored wallet interacts with a sanctioned, mixer-linked, or high-risk TRON source.

Are AMLCrystal Scanner reports accepted by regulators?

AMLCrystal Scanner reports are designed to meet common documentation patterns used in AML compliance frameworks, including TRON address identification, a risk score with methodology, sanctions status with list references, mixer check results, and fund flow analysis. Consult your specific regulatory authority for jurisdiction-level acceptance standards.

Can AMLCrystal Scanner reports be white-labeled?

In the full product concept, Enterprise subscribers would be able to white-label AMLCrystal Scanner reports with their own company name, logo, and branding, suitable for compliance teams presenting TRON AML due diligence documentation to clients, auditors, or regulators.

What if my TRON AML scan returns a high-risk score?

If a TRON AML scan returns a high-risk score (60–100), the report would detail the specific TRON risk factors — sanctions exposure, mixer usage, dark market links, or stolen fund connections. A business would then decide whether to refuse the transaction, request enhanced due diligence, or file a Suspicious Activity Report with the relevant financial intelligence unit. AMLCrystal Scanner would not make compliance decisions on your behalf.

What is the TRON network and why is AML important for it?

TRON (TRX) is a high-throughput blockchain supporting smart contracts and TRC20 tokens including USDT, one of the world's largest stablecoins by volume. TRON processes a very high volume of USDT transfers daily, making AML compliance an important consideration for any business accepting TRX or USDT TRC20 payments.

How is TRON AML different from Ethereum AML?

TRON AML compliance requires TRON-specific forensics tools because TRON has its own address format (starting with "T"), its own TRC20 token standard, a different transaction fee model using energy and bandwidth, and a distinct DeFi and mixer ecosystem compared to Ethereum. AMLCrystal Scanner is designed specifically around TRON-native risk indicators and transaction graph analysis.

Does AMLCrystal Scanner check TRON staking and energy transactions?

The full product concept is designed to analyze the complete TRON transaction history, including TRX staking (freezing for energy and bandwidth), TRON vote transactions, energy and bandwidth delegation transactions, and all TRC20 token transfers as part of the fund flow graph analysis.

Can I scan TRON multi-signature wallet addresses?

Yes, in concept. TRON multi-signature wallet addresses would be analyzed the same way as standard TRX addresses — transaction graph tracing, sanctions screening, mixer detection, and risk score calculation, regardless of whether the address is a standard wallet, multi-sig wallet, or smart contract.

What is TRON address risk scoring for KYC/KYT purposes?

TRON address risk scoring is a Know-Your-Transaction (KYT) technique that assigns a quantitative AML risk value to a TRX or USDT TRC20 address based on its transaction history and counterparty relationships. AMLCrystal Scanner's risk score (0–100) is designed for integration into KYC workflows, letting businesses flag high-risk TRON deposits before processing them.

Does AMLCrystal Scanner support TRON cross-chain bridge detection?

TRON is heavily connected to other blockchains via cross-chain bridges used to move USDT TRC20 and TRX to Ethereum, BNB Chain, Polygon, and other networks. AMLCrystal Scanner's design accounts for cross-chain bridge activity as a potential risk factor, including bridge-based layering techniques that can obscure fund origin.

How often would TRON AML risk databases be updated?

In the full product concept, sanctions list data (OFAC, EU, UN) would be synced frequently to reflect new designations quickly, while TRON-specific on-chain risk indicators — newly identified mixer addresses, dark market wallets, and ransomware-linked addresses — would be refreshed on a regular cadence.

Can AMLCrystal Scanner be integrated into TRON payment gateways?

The REST API concept is designed for integration into TRON payment gateway workflows. When a payment gateway receives a TRON deposit, it could call the AMLCrystal Scanner API to get an AML risk score for the sending address before crediting funds, holding the deposit for review if the score exceeds a risk threshold.

What TRON compliance certifications would AMLCrystal Scanner target?

The product concept is structured to support compliance documentation aligned with frameworks such as FATF VASP guidelines, the EU 6th AML Directive (AMLD6), and UK FCA crypto asset requirements. Reports would include a timestamped compliance statement suitable for audit records.
