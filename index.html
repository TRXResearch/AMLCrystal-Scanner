FAQ

TRON AML Questions

Everything about TRON AML verification, TRX risk scoring, USDT TRC20 sanctions screening, and how AML Crystal Scanner works.

What is AML Crystal Scanner?

TRON blockchain AML compliance platform. Checks TRX wallets, USDT TRC20 addresses, and TRON transactions for AML risk, sanctions, and mixer activity. Crystal Reports from 0.10 USDT.

How does AML Crystal Scanner work?

Analyzes TRON transaction history, traces fund flows up to 10 hops, screens sanctions lists, detects mixer usage, and computes a Crystal Risk Score (0–100).

Which TRON tokens are supported?

TRX, USDT TRC20, USDC TRC20, WTRX, JST, SUN, BTT, WIN, and all TRC20 standard tokens on TRON Mainnet.

How much does a TRON AML scan cost?

Single scans from $0.10 USDT. Bulk packs from $0.05 per scan. API plans from $0.02 per call. Enterprise custom pricing available.

What does the Crystal Risk Score mean?

0–25: Low risk. 26–59: Medium risk — enhanced due diligence needed. 60–100: High risk — sanctions, mixer, or dark market exposure.

Does it check USDT TRC20 specifically?

Yes. Dedicated USDT TRC20 AML check with full TRON-layer transfer history, counterparty risk, layering patterns, and sanctions screening.

What sanctions lists are screened?

OFAC SDN, OFAC Consolidated, EU Consolidated, UN Security Council, UK HM Treasury, and FATF High-Risk Jurisdictions.

How accurate is the risk detection?

Built on documented sanctions data and TRON-specific risk patterns. Updated continuously. Not a substitute for professional compliance judgment.

How long does a scan take?

Typically under 2 seconds for most TRX wallets and USDT TRC20 addresses. Larger histories may take longer.

Is there a REST API?

Yes. Supports single and batch TRON address scans, webhooks, JSON/CSV/PDF export, and TRON transaction-level AML checks.

Who should use AML Crystal Scanner?

TRON exchanges, USDT TRC20 payment processors, TRON DeFi platforms, OTC desks, P2P exchanges, and compliance teams.

Can it detect TRON mixer usage?

Yes. Checks for known TRON mixing patterns, TRON-native privacy tools, and chain-hopping techniques. Mixer exposure is a high-weight factor.

Is it aligned with FATF guidelines?

Designed with reference to FATF guidance for virtual asset service providers (VASPs). Supports CDD and EDD workflows.

What is direct vs indirect sanctions exposure?

Direct: address appears on a sanctions list. Indirect: address has transacted with a sanctioned TRON entity within the analyzed graph.

Can it analyze TRON DeFi wallets?

Yes. Supports wallets interacting with JustLend, JustSwap, SunSwap, and other TRON DeFi protocols. Counterparty risk analysis included.

How does real-time monitoring work?

Subscribes a TRX or USDT TRC20 address for ongoing surveillance. Alerts triggered when funds interact with sanctioned or high-risk sources.

Are reports regulator-ready?

Includes address, risk score with methodology, sanctions status with list references, mixer results, and timestamp. Confirm with your compliance advisor.

Can reports be white-labeled?

Enterprise subscribers can request white-labeled PDFs with their own branding. White-label API responses also available.

What if a scan returns high risk?

Report details the specific factors — sanctions, mixer matches, dark market links, flagged sources. Use as one input into your compliance decision.

Does it protect user privacy?

Analyzes public TRON on-chain data only. No private keys, passwords, or credentials requested. Scan history tied to authenticated account.

Is there a free trial?

New accounts can request trial scan credits. Sample Crystal Report PDF available without registration. After trial, scans from $0.10 USDT.

Why does TRON need AML?

TRON carries massive USDT volume. AML screening helps businesses avoid unknowingly accepting funds from sanctioned entities, mixers, or high-risk sources.

How is TRON AML different from Ethereum AML?

TRON has its own address format, TRC20 standard, fee model, and DeFi ecosystem. AML Crystal Scanner is built specifically for TRON-native risk indicators.

Does it check staking and energy transactions?

Yes. Covers TRX staking, vote transactions, energy/bandwidth delegation, and TRC20 transfers in the fund flow graph.

Can it scan multi-signature wallets?

Yes. Analyzed the same as standard TRX addresses — graph tracing, sanctions, mixer detection, and risk score calculation.

What is TRON address risk scoring for KYC/KYT?

Quantitative AML risk value for TRX/USDT addresses based on transaction history and counterparty relationships. Plug into KYC/KYT workflows.

Does it detect cross-chain bridges?

Factors TRON cross-chain bridge activity (to Ethereum, BNB Chain, Polygon) into risk assessment, including bridge-based layering.

How often is risk data updated?

Sanctions lists refreshed regularly. TRON-specific risk indicators reviewed and updated continuously based on available intelligence.

Can it be integrated into payment gateways?

Yes. REST API integrates into TRON payment workflows — call API to get Crystal Risk Score before crediting funds.

What frameworks does it support?

FATF VASP guidance and general CDD/EDD practices. Always confirm specific requirements with a qualified compliance professional.

Is AML Crystal Scanner a regulated institution?

No. A blockchain analytics and AML screening tool providing informational risk data based on public TRON on-chain data.

Does a low risk score guarantee safety?

No. Low score means no known risk indicators matched at scan time — not a guarantee. New risks may not yet be reflected. Use as part of broader due diligence.
